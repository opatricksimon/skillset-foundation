# PRODUCT VISION
## Skillset company and product direction

> **Version:** 0.1.0
> **Updated:** 2026-05-25
> **Status:** Living document

---

## Summary

Skillset is an international online course marketplace for independent experts who want to publish serious educational programs without building their own website, stitching together tools, or managing payment infrastructure alone.

The core product promise is simple:

**Experts publish structured courses. Learners buy, study, interact, and receive verifiable credentials. Skillset handles the platform layer.**

---

## Problem

Independent experts usually face one of two bad choices:

- Use a general course platform that feels generic, crowded, and weak on creator trust.
- Build their own stack with landing pages, video hosting, payments, email, community, support, and certificates.

Both options create friction before the expert can sell a serious course.

---

## Target Customers

| Customer | Need | Skillset Value |
|----------|------|----------------|
| Independent educators | Publish and sell a structured course | Course builder, checkout, review, payouts, and learner access. |
| Professional experts | Turn expertise into a business asset | Premium presentation, global checkout, and verifiable credentials. |
| Learners | Find serious courses with more trust | Reviewed courses, clear curriculum, community, and certificates. |
| Operators/admins | Keep the marketplace safe | Review, moderation, support, and payment visibility. |

---

## Product Principles

- No fake marketplace data.
- Course publishing must be real, not decorative.
- Payment flows must be explicit and trustworthy.
- Teacher and learner surfaces must be clearly separated.
- The product should feel premium even when metrics are zero.
- Public claims must match the current product reality.

---

## Strategic Positioning

Skillset sits between self-serve creator platforms and high-trust professional learning marketplaces.

| Platform Type | Strength | Weakness | Skillset Direction |
|---------------|----------|----------|--------------------|
| Creator checkout tools | Fast monetization | Often weak learning experience | Add serious course structure and credentials. |
| Course marketplaces | Existing demand | Hard for creators to control brand and data | Give creators a clearer studio and promise. |
| All-in-one course tools | Flexible | Creator must bring all demand and operations | Add marketplace review and discovery layer. |

---

## Near-Term Product Thesis

The MVP wins if one teacher can:

1. Sign up.
2. Complete onboarding.
3. Create a course.
4. Add modules and lessons.
5. Upload or embed lesson content.
6. Submit the course for review.
7. Receive a paid enrollment.
8. Have a learner watch the course.

The investor story becomes stronger only after this loop is working end to end.
